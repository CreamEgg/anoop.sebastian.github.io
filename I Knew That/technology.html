20% of divorces are because of Facebook

When your girlfriend insists on friending you on Facebook and you’re not for the idea, she may be trying to salvage your relationship – well, and marking her territory, of course. The social networking site has been blamed for rising cases of marital fallouts.

Google tried to sell itself for $1 million in 1999

Would you believe Google wanted to cash in for $1 million in its early days? True, and what’s also true is that no one was interested in buying it. One company that stepped forth was Excite, and the CEO at the time, George Bell, said he wasn’t willing to pay a million dollars for the search engine. He haggled with the search engine’s founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin to bring the price down to $750,000, but he ended up rejecting that offer as well!

The first eBay auction was for a broken laser pointer

eBay was founded in 1995 as AuctionWeb, the same year that Amazon rolled out its service. What did the first auction entail? It was for a broken laser pointer that fetched at $14.83.

The CD was designed so it could hold Beethoven’s Ninth Symphony!

Norio Ohga is credited for being the ‘father of the CD’, and he died in April 2011 aged 81. Ohga served at one time as the CEO of Sony and Chairman of the board and this was between the years 1982-1995, stepping down as president in ’95 but went on to serve as chairman until 2003.

Everything you say to Siri, Apple will save it on its servers

Did you know what the word ‘Siri’ means in Swahili? It means secret. But this is not the case with Apple’s Siri app, because, Big Brother is listening. Whenever you whisper to Siri, everything you say ends up on the tech giant’s servers. Apple revealed that the voice prompts are stored for as long as two years.

Over 6 billion text messages are sent every day

The likes of instant messaging may be growing in use, but short messaging service, otherwise known as SMS, still carries the bulk of mobile interactions. It would help matters if we pointed out that the 6-billion figure is consumption by American mobile users only. Astounding, right?

The Windows 95 start up music was composed on a Mac

Some interesting tech fact you probably didn’t know about. Those who are old enough remember the memorable start-up tune of Windows 95 very well. The composer of the music Brian Eno in a radio interview revealed that he actually composed the music on an Apple Macintosh.

Facebook can see every site you visit while browsing the web but only when you’re logged into your FB account

Facebook can still track where you are, even if you log out. Facebook, for its part, has denied the claims.

Kim Dotcom had not just been jet-setting around the globe, or amazing new sports cars, or just hanging out with Bruce Willis. Search More Breathe Less

One google search produces about 0.2g of CO2. But since you hardly get an answer from one search, a typical search session produces about the same amount of CO2 as does boiling a kettle.

Fastest Internet Speed

Hong kong is the worlds fastest internet place. It has blazing fast internet, an average peak speed of 54.1 megabits per seconds. At this speed you can download HD movie in about 4 minutes.

Free Internet for the Entire Planet?

Outernet, hopes to launch hundreds of tiny satellites—known as CubeSats—to provide Internet to every person on Earth. Forty percent of the world’s people currently don’t have access to the Web. In a little more than a year, Outernet plans to have a fleet of 24 satellites operational and testing to pave the way for a globe-spanning network.
